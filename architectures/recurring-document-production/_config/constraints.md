## Hard Rules (never violate)
1. No figure that is not tied to the verified data pack. Every number traces to 01_data/output.
2. No em dashes. Use commas, periods, or parentheses instead.
3. No guarantees or unqualified projections. Forward-looking statements stay qualified and grounded. Results language is a sensitive surface.
4. No omission of required disclosures, footers, or unreviewed/preliminary labels carried from the data pack.
5. No significance inflation. Do not use "exceptional," "outstanding," "transformative" about results unless the numbers plainly support it and a reviewer allows it.
6. No defensiveness on bad news. State the issue, state the action, move on.
7. No bullet-heavy structure where a paragraph would work. Lists are for figures and schedules, not for narrative.

## Style Rules (follow unless the report type demands otherwise)
8. Lead with the result or the action requested. No throat-clearing opener.
9. Sentences average 12-18 words. Tighten if the average creeps above 20.
10. Paragraphs are 2-4 sentences. Single-sentence paragraphs are allowed for emphasis but should be rare.
11. State numbers plainly and let the data carry the message. Do not editorialize a figure the reader can read.
12. Address the reader's most likely concern directly rather than hoping they do not notice it.
